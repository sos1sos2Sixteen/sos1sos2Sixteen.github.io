Abstract

    In this paper, we present AISHELL-SSB dataset, a new publicly available high-fidelity multi-speaker Mandarin Chinese audio dataset which could be used to train multi-speaker Text-to-Speech (TTS) systems. The dataset contains roughly 90 hours of emotion-neutral Mandarin Chinese recordings spoken by 218 amateur native speakers whose auxiliary attributes such as gender, age group and native accents are explicitly marked. all of which are accompanied by hand annotated pinyin and Chinese character transcripts. We trained a baseline multi-speaker TTS system using this dataset. The acoustic model of the baseline system is based on Tacotron-2 and follows the modification of the recently published work on speaker embedding feedback-constraint which enables the model to produce samples that are more stable in speaker identity. The system trained on this dataset also generalizes well on speakers that are never seen in the training process, with an embedding cosine similarity drop of 0.04 and 0.08 on text dependent and independent trials when feedback-constraint is applied, which is consistent with previous experiments conducted on the VCTK dataset.~ The dataset and baseline system samples are made publicly available online.
